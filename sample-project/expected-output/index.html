Behind the Panels

How a 10-codon AI pipeline built a comic from 20 lines of text

20 lines → 12 pages of comic → $52.58 total cost → 123 min total time
ideas.md — the entire input

# shadowSentience

Hello, we are the people who created Hankweave. A lot of people don't know about Hankweave yet, so we'd like to turn to a few of our favorite sci-fi short stories, and invite folks to ponder about hankweave.

1. "The Nine Billion Names of God" (Clarke)

2. "The Last Question" (Asimov)

3. Hankweave

## What's the story about?

Tell the story of The Nine Billion Names of God and The Last Question. Weave Hankweave there as if it's a third story — a fourth wall break.

## Style

Mystical, handrawn, beautiful, artistic — one that reminds one of the starry night, mysterious, awe-inspiring.

Act IV — Quality

The Rerun Package

The pipeline's last gift: the ability to fix one panel without losing the rest.

output/rerun/ — regeneration toolkit
  • $ ls output/rerun/
  • prompts/ ← per-panel generation prompts
  • scripts/ ← generation + post-processing tools
  • cover-reference.png ← character consistency anchor
  • fix-panel.sh ← regenerate specific panels
  • regenerate.sh ← regenerate everything
  • spec.md ← 29K-word production spec
  • $ ./fix-panel.sh panel-05 panel-09
  • Regenerating panel-05...
  • Regenerating panel-09...
  • Re-running post-processing pipeline...
  • Done! Check: output/comic.pdf
12 prompt files
~$2 per panel fix
~3 min per panel

This is the part that makes me proudest.

The pipeline didn't just make a comic. It left behind everything you'd need to fix the comic. Every panel has its own prompt file. Every prompt references the cover for character consistency. The post-processing pipeline is self-contained.

Panel 5 had a truncated caption — "The Rec" instead of "The Recovery." Panel 9 needed more void-darkness. Both were regenerated in minutes, without touching the other ten panels.

This is Hankweave's deepest promise: not that the first run is perfect, but that fixing is cheap and precise. A scalpel, not a sledgehammer.
