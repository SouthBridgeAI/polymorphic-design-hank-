# Character Option A: Kavi the Loomfly

## Who Are They?

A **silkworm moth** (Bombyx mori) named **Kavi** - small, soft-furred, with enormous feathery antennae that glow faintly at the tips - who weaves threads of light between stories, connecting patterns no one else can see.

## Why This Creature?

The silkworm moth is the only fully domesticated insect on Earth. It cannot survive in the wild - it exists because of its partnership with humans. It has been cultivated for over 5,000 years, and its single purpose is to weave: to produce silk thread, endlessly, patiently, one strand at a time.

This maps directly onto Hankweave's core identity:
- **Weaving** is literally in the product name (Hank*weave*)
- The silkworm is a creature that **cannot exist without collaboration** - it is the ultimate architect-agent partnership
- Silk production is a process of **patient accumulation** - one continuous thread, up to 1,500 meters per cocoon, built strand by strand. Like codons building a pipeline.
- The cocoon is **amber** - it preserves the creature inside, protects the transformation happening within. "Hankweave is amber. Your working AI workflow is the mosquito." Except here: the moth IS the amber AND the thing being preserved.
- Silk moths navigate by starlight. Their antennae detect the faintest signals. They find patterns in noise.

The connection to the source stories: monks systematically enumerate names (weaving a list), AC systematically processes data across eons (weaving an answer from chaos). Kavi weaves between all three stories because weaving IS the shared act.

## Visual Design

**Shape:** Round, soft, compact body - like a cotton ball with wings. Head slightly oversized for expressiveness. Plush toy proportions: 60% body, 25% head, 15% wings.

**Proportions:** About the size of a human fist. Small enough to perch on a shoulder, large enough to be the center of a panel. Wings fold like a shawl when resting.

**Color:** Cream-white body with silver-grey markings. Wings are translucent with faint constellations visible through them - as if the night sky is printed on the wing membranes. Antennae are feathery, delicate, and glow a soft amber-gold at the tips.

**Signature Accessory:** A single spool of luminous thread worn at the waist like a belt or sash. The thread is the same amber-gold as the antennae tips. When Kavi weaves, the thread leaves visible lines in the air - like fiber-optic cables made of starlight.

**Silhouette Treatment:** In the Reiniger silhouette style, Kavi reads as a distinctive rounded shape with two dramatic feathery antennae and delicate wing outlines. The glow at the antennae tips would be rendered as the ONLY color within the silhouette - tiny amber dots, like stars inside the shadow.

## Emotional Range

1. **Default (Curious):** Antennae tilted forward, both at the same angle. Wings loosely folded. Eyes (simple dots) centered. Slight head tilt. The posture of someone listening carefully.

2. **Happy (Discovering a Pattern):** Antennae fan wide and upward, like exclamation marks. Wings flutter slightly open, revealing constellation patterns. Whole body lifts on tiptoes. The spool at the waist spins, trailing light.

3. **Sad (A Thread Breaks):** Antennae droop forward over the face, like bangs. Wings wrap around body like a blanket. Body curls inward. The glow at the antennae tips dims to a faint flicker.

4. **Determined (Weaving Through Difficulty):** Antennae swept back, aerodynamic. Wings fully extended. Body leans forward. Thread pulled taut from the spool, held in two tiny front legs. Eyes narrowed (dots become dashes). The posture of someone pulling a heavy thread through resistant fabric.

5. **Surprised (The Stars Do Something Unexpected):** Antennae shoot straight up, perfectly vertical. Wings snap open symmetrically. Body jolts backward slightly. The glow intensifies to bright gold. All the constellations on the wings rearrange.

## The One Image That Sells Them

**The panel:** Kavi sits on the edge of a Tibetan monastery parapet at twilight, silhouetted against a sky full of stars. The monastery is rendered in the Reiniger style - ornate architectural silhouette with prayer wheels and stupas. Below and behind, the Mark V computer hums in a lit window, golden light spilling out.

Kavi's antennae are extended, and from each tip, luminous threads reach outward - one thread arcs up to connect to a star (which is actually a name of God, rendered in the monks' special alphabet), another thread reaches down to the computer's golden glow, and a third reaches forward, toward the reader, toward the real world, toward Hankweave.

The thread makes a web. The web is a constellation. The constellation is a pattern. And Kavi, sitting in the middle of it all, is barely bigger than a teacup. But the web spans the entire sky.

**Caption:** "She couldn't make the stars stay lit. But she could show you how they connected."
