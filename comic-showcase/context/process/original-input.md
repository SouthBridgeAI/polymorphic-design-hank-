# shadowSentience

Hello, we are the people who created Hankweave. A lot of people don't know about Hankewave yet, so we'd like to turn to a few of our favorite sci-fi short stories, and invite folks to ponder about hankweave. I'd like for you to think about ways to introduce these awesome stories and think about hankweave in their light - would be great to get us excited, even trigger that awe for the unknown that we are building for - because we often feel that way. 

1. **"The Nine Billion Names of God"** (Clarke) 
2. **"The Last Question"** (Asimov) 
3. **Hankweave** 

## What's the story about?

In whatever creative narrative technique you have in mind, tell the story of The Nine Billion Names of God and The Last Question. I know we have limited space, but tell it - they both have a impact. Weave hankweave there as if it's a third story, and a real one - a fourth wall break. 

## Style

Mystical, handrawn, beautiful, artistic - one that remind one of the starry night, mysterious, awe-inspiring. .
